# COURSE OVERVIEW

This course provides a comprehensive understanding of the principles, technologies, and applications of sensors and actuators in the context of the Internet of Things (IoT). Students will explore various types of sensors and actuators, learn how to interface them with IoT devices, and understand the data acquisition, processing, and control mechanisms required to build robust IoT systems. Practical lab sessions and projects will give hands-on experience with real-world IoT applications.

# COURSE OBJECTIVE (S)

The objectives of this course are to:

- Understand the fundamental concepts and types of sensors and actuators used in IoT.
- Learn about the interfacing techniques and protocols for integrating sensors and actuators with IoT devices.
- Develop skills to design and implement IoT systems incorporating sensor data acquisition and actuator control.
- Analyze and process sensor data for effective decision-making in IoT applications.
- Explore the use of sensors and actuators in various IoT domains, such as smart homes, healthcare, industrial automation, and environmental monitoring.

**2. Semiconductor junction-based thermal sensors operate based on the principle that:**
- A) Resistance increases linearly with temperature
- B) Voltage drop across a PN junction varies with temperature
- C) Capacitance changes inversely with temperature
- D) Inductance becomes negligible at high temperatures
- **Answer: B**

**3. What is the primary advantage of using a Helium Low Temperature Thermometer?**

- B) It can measure extremely low temperatures near absolute zero
- C) It is immune to electromagnetic interference

- A) Radiation emitted by radioactive materials

- **Answer: B**

**5. A Magnetic Thermometer works on the principle that:**
- A) Magnetic field strength is proportional to temperature
- B) Thermal expansion of materials changes magnetic properties

- D) Temperature causes ferromagnetic materials to become paramagnetic
- **Answer: C**

- **Answer: C**
